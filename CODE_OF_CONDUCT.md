# **Code of Conduct**

## **1. Purpose**

To maintain a collaborative, safe, and respectful environment, all contributors, maintainers, and users of this project must follow the principles outlined in this Code of Conduct.
The goal is to ensure that every interaction within the project—whether through code contributions, issue discussions, documentation updates, or community conversations—is conducted professionally and constructively.

---

## **2. Expected Behaviour**

All participants are expected to:

### **2.1 Respect Others**

* Treat all individuals with courtesy, consideration, and respect.
* Value differing viewpoints and experiences.
* Assume good intentions during discussions and reviews.

### **2.2 Maintain Professional Standards**

* Communicate clearly and constructively.
* Provide thoughtful, evidence-based feedback.
* Use appropriate language; avoid insults, sarcasm used to belittle others, or dismissive behaviour.
* Follow project guidelines, including coding standards, documentation practices, and submission procedures.

### **2.3 Promote an Inclusive Environment**

* Welcome contributions from people of all backgrounds, disciplines, and levels of experience.
* Avoid discriminatory, exclusionary, or offensive conduct.

### **2.4 Demonstrate Responsibility**

* Acknowledge and correct mistakes promptly.
* Credit the work of others fairly.
* Ensure that contributions are original, ethical, and compliant with data-handling requirements.

---

## **3. Unacceptable Behaviour**

The following behaviours are not tolerated:

* Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory remarks relating to gender, race, ethnicity, nationality, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or any personal characteristics.
* Threats, intimidation, or personal attacks.
* Posting or distributing sensitive, confidential, or private data.
* Excessive profanity or offensive humour.
* Knowingly introducing security vulnerabilities or harmful code.
* Persistent disruption of discussions or undermining project goals.

---

## **4. Reporting Violations**

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. Document relevant details (messages, screenshots, context).
2. Contact the project maintainer or open a confidential report via the project’s issue tracker or designated communication channel.

Reports will be handled sensitively and professionally.
Retaliation against individuals who report concerns in good faith is strictly prohibited.

---

## **5. Enforcement**

Project maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.
They may take action they deem appropriate, including:

* Requesting a public or private apology
* Requiring behavioural modifications
* Removing comments, issues, or pull requests
* Temporary or permanent suspension from contributing
* Escalation to organisational or institutional authorities (if applicable)

Enforcement decisions will be fair, consistent, and proportionate to the circumstances.

---

## **6. Maintainer Responsibilities**

Maintainers must:

* Model exemplary behaviour in all interactions
* Review contributions objectively and impartially
* Provide guidance without hostility or condescension
* Protect the community from toxic behaviour
* Uphold technical and ethical standards of the project

Maintainers may decline contributions that fail to align with project requirements or compromise the project's integrity.

---

## **7. Data Ethics and Privacy**

Given that this project involves **real-world crime datasets and geospatial analysis**, contributors must:

* Avoid uploading or distributing protected, sensitive, or personally identifiable information
* Ensure that all data handling complies with local privacy regulations
* Use aggregated or anonymised datasets wherever possible
* Avoid inappropriate inference or misuse of data related to individuals or communities

Ethical considerations must guide all modelling, interpretation, and publication efforts.

---

## **8. Scope**

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

* GitHub issues and pull requests
* Documentation and discussions
* Dash application development
* Communication via email or project channels
* Offline project-related interactions

---

## **9. Attribution**

This Code of Conduct draws inspiration from established open-source standards such as the **Contributor Covenant** and internal organisational conduct policies, adapted to the needs of this project.

---

## **10. Agreement**

By contributing to this repository, you indicate that you:

* Have read and understood this Code of Conduct
* Agree to comply with its principles
* Accept the enforcement process carried out by project maintainers
